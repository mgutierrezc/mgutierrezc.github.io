About

I am a Peruvian economist working as a Pre-Doctoral Fellow in Economics at Columbia University in the City of New York. The projects I'm currently involved in are related to development and behavioral economics, corporate finance, and natural language processing.

My current work involves applying natural language processing to obtain insights about judicial decisions in Peru, and corporate social responsibility in India, and to develop new tools to support public officers in Peru in their work against corruption.

In the past years, I've also collaborated on building the second laboratory of experimental economics in my country, as well as supporting professors from Brown University, , World Bank, George Mason University, and the University of California on behavioral and experimental economics projects.

I have worked intensively with Python for backend programming of web applications for small and large-scale experiments as well as natural language processing, as well as Bash for creating tools for behavioral economists interested in running laboratory experiments, and to run computationally intensive projects on a server cluster. My experience also includes working with Amazon Web Services (EC2, RDS, MTurk), Heroku, managing Ubuntu servers and using Google Cloud APIs. I'm also well-versed in algorithmics, time and memory efficiency, and object-oriented programming.

Looking for a Ph.D./MSc that allows me to do research at the intersection of economics and data science.
